Aristotle

The great philosopher

BIOGRAPHY

  • 384 BC

    Aristotle was born in 384 BC in Stagira, Chalcidice. While he was young, learned about biology and medical information, which was taught by his father.

  • 367/366 BC

    At the age of seventeen or eighteen, Aristotle moved to Athens to continue his education at Plato's Academy.

  • 348/347 BC

    After Plato's death, Aristotle leaves Athens and moves with other colleagues to Assos (today located on the coast of Turkey). During this period, Aristotle founded his first philosophical school and eventually marries Pithias, daughter of Hermeias, king of Assos and who also attended Plato's Academy. Aristotle has a daughter who, like her mother, is also called Pithias.

  • 344 BC

    Herméias is deposed. Aristotle moves to Mytilene on the islands of Lesbos. He associates with Theophrastos, a native of this city and also a graduate of Plato's academy and makes important studies in biology.

  • 343 BC

    Philip, king of Macedonia, invites Aristotle to live in his residence and be the tutor of his son Alexander who is 13 years old.

  • 335 BC

    Philip dies. Alexander ascends the throne. Aristotle returns to Athens and founds his own school, the Lyceum. During this period Pythias dies and Aristotle marries Herpyllis who was also a native of Stagira. With her Aristotle has a son named Nicomachus.

  • 323 BC

    After extending his conquests to Egypt, Syria, Persia and India, Alexander the Great Dies (in India); Because of anti-Macedonian sentiment, Aristotle is forced to leave Athens for the last time.

  • 322 BC

    Aristotle dies in Chalsis, in Euboea.
